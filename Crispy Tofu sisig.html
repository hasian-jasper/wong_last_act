Crispy Tofu Sisig

A Filipino Crispy Tofu Sisig cooked with an aromatic blend of onions, garlic, peppers, and chiles. Crispy tofu is used in place of meat but don’t let that fool you; it’s crispy, delicious, and easy to prepare. This is a hearty vegan take on the Filipino Kapampangan classic!


Ingridients

  • 10 pieces Tofu sliced into small cubes
  • 1 1/2 Canola oil
  • 5 pieces Thai Chili chopped
  • 2 Long Green and Red bell pepper chopped
  • 1 Medium Yellow Onion chopped
  • 1 Onion, peeled and chopped
  • 3 Cloves Garlic, peeled and minced
  • 10 tablespoons Vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon Cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon Butter
  • 1/2 teaspoon Onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon Soy sauce
  • Salt and ground Black pepper to taste
  • 2 Thai chili peppers
  • 1/4 cup Calamansi juice
  • 1/4 cup Knorr seasoning sauce
  • 1/4 cup Mayonnaise

Instructions

  1. In a large pan over medium heat, heat oil to a depth of about 2 inches until it is hot but not smoking.
  2. Tofu should be added in batches and cooked until golden and crisp, turning as necessary. Remove from pan with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
  3. In a bowl, mix cornstarch, soy sauce, onion powder, and vegetable broth. Stir all the ingredients together thoroughly. Place aside.
  4. Melt about 1 tablespoon of butter in a skillet over medium heat. Cook the onions and garlic with the addition until just softened.
  5. Take 3 tablespoons of the oil that was used to fry the tofu. In a clean pan, pour it. On a stovetop, heat the pan, add the onion, long green peppers, and chili, and cook until crisp-tender.
  6. Add the crispy fried tofu. Stir-fry for 1 minute.
  7. In the pan, pour the bowl containing the broth mixture. Mixture-coated ingredients are ready after being tossed. Add the mayonnaise, salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Transfer to a serving plate. Serve with a dipping sauce composed of soy sauce, calamansi, and crushed chili peppers.
